## Course description

A brief introduction to relational databases and SQL for students interested
in data analysis. The course covers ER modeling, selection and filtering
queries, joins, aggregates, subqueries and CTEs, and window functions.
Database administration topics (indexing, transactions, stored procedures)
are out of scope.

**Platform:** Google BigQuery, accessed from Google Colab.
**Datasets:** `nyu-datasets.imdb` and `nyu-datasets.facebook`.

## Learning outcomes

By the end of the course, students will be able to:

1. Read an ER diagram and translate it into a relational schema.
2. Write `SELECT` queries with filtering, ordering, and conditional logic.
3. Combine tables with inner, outer, semi-, and anti-joins.
4. Summarize data with aggregates, `GROUP BY`, and `HAVING`.
5. Decompose complex problems with subqueries and CTEs.
6. Use window functions for ranking, offsets, and rolling aggregations.
